Our Story

Founded in 2010, the Historical Reenactment Group began as a small gathering of history enthusiasts who shared a common vision: to make the past accessible, engaging, and educational for modern audiences. What started with just a dozen members has grown into a thriving community of over 200 dedicated reenactors spanning multiple historical periods.

Our journey began in the rolling hills of Sherwood Forest, where our first medieval encampment drew curious visitors and sparked a movement. Today, we operate across the United Kingdom, bringing authentic historical experiences to thousands of people each year.

We believe that history shouldn't be confined to books and museums. Through careful research, authentic costumes, and passionate storytelling, we create immersive experiences that transport visitors back in time.

Our Mission & Values

Authenticity

We are committed to historical accuracy in every aspect of our reenactments, from costumes and weapons to social customs and daily life.

Education

We strive to make history engaging and accessible, providing educational opportunities for people of all ages and backgrounds.

Community

We foster a supportive, inclusive community where members can share knowledge, develop skills, and form lasting friendships.

Preservation

We work to preserve historical techniques, crafts, and traditions that might otherwise be lost to time.

Historical Periods We Represent

Ancient Civilizations

  • Ancient Greece (5th Century BC)
  • Roman Empire (1st-3rd Century AD)
  • Ancient Egypt (New Kingdom)
  • Celtic Tribes

Experience the foundations of Western civilization through authentic demonstrations of philosophy, warfare, and daily life.

Medieval Period

  • Early Medieval (5th-10th Century)
  • High Medieval (11th-13th Century)
  • Late Medieval (14th-15th Century)
  • Viking Age (8th-11th Century)

Step into the world of knights, castles, and feudal society with our comprehensive medieval program.

Renaissance & Beyond

  • Renaissance (14th-17th Century)
  • Colonial America (17th-18th Century)
  • Victorian Era (19th Century)
  • Early Modern Period

Explore the rebirth of culture, colonial expansion, and industrial revolution through living history.
